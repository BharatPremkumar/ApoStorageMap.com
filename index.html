1.Prioritering av stabling

Stacking of goods must be prioritized in the following order: 160, 170, 190 ... and so on. Think of the numbers as indicating times for when they should be fully stacked. It is important to always try to keep the goods within the pallet size in both width and height.

2. Stabling av kjølevarer

Refrigerated goods can either be collected from the refrigerated counter (see the map) or from the stations' belts (black boxes). These items usually have a significant weight (10-20 kg), so they should ideally be placed at the bottom of the pallet or on top of items that can withstand the load.

3.Oslovarer som skal stables

In the evening, 4 deliveries to Oslo will be sorted: 40A, 41B, 41C and 70C. These must be handled by the evening guards, and it is also up to them to decide where and when they will be stacked.

3.Plasting og etiketter på varer

Before the goods are sent, most must be plasticized and have a label affixed to the front or back of the pallet. It is crucial that the label is clearly visible and is not hidden by the plastic. However, there are some items that should not be plasticized or have a label.

Items that NOT should be plasticized: 60A, 70C and 220

Items that SHOULD be plasticized, but NOT have a label: 40A, 50(A,B), 60A, 70(A, C), 220, 160(A, B, C), 170/180C and TROA/TROB
